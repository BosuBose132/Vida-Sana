Common Food Replacement

Explore simple vegan alternatives for common non-vegan foods and learn how they can be used in daily meals.

Milk

alternatives: Almond milk, soy milk, oat milk

Note: Useful as a diary-free replacement in drinks, cereals and cooking.

Meat

Alternative: Tofu, tempeh, lentils

Note: Great palnt-based options that provide protein and work well in many savory dishes.

Yogurt

Alternative: Coconut yogurt, soy yogurt

Note: A creany dairy-free option that can be enjoyed as a snack or used in breakfast meals.

Butter

Alternative: Vegan butter, oilve oil

Note: Works well for cooking, baking, and spreading while avoiding dairy.

Cheese

Alternative: Cashew cheese, nutritional yeast.

Note: These alternatives can add creamy or cheesy flavour to meals without dairy.

Eggs

Alternative: Flax egg, chia egg, tofu scramble.

Note: Helpful replacements for baking, scrambling, and binding ingredients in recipes.

Chicken

Alternative: Tofu, seitan, jackfruit

Note: Popular plant based substitutes that mimic texture in savory dishes.

Fish

Alternative: Banana blosom, tofu

Note: Can replicate flaky texture and work well in fried or curry recipes.

Honey

Alternative: Maple syrup, agave nectar

Note: Natutral sweetners that work well in beverages and deserts.

Cream

Alternative: Coconut cream, cashew cream

Note: Provides a rich and creamy texture for sauses and deserts.

Mayonnaises

Alternative: Vegan mayo, mashed avocado

Note: Works well as a spread or dressing without using eggs.

Gelatin

Alternative: Agar agar

Note: A plant-based thickening agent used in deserts and jellies.

Tips for choosing vegan replacements
  • • Check nutrition labels before buying
  • • Choose fortified plant milk when possible
  • • Start with simple replacements first
  • • Pick options that fit your allergies and preferences
